Altitude Associated Changes in ant Morphology and Genus-dependent Resource Preference in Guadarrama Ants

Description: Statistical Analysis on the morphology and eating preference ants

Our study took place in the Guadarra Mountains of Spain. Scientists measured the size of the different morphological structures of a multitude of ant genera across varying altitudes and regions of Guadarra mountain range as well as the resources ants were observed using. We aim to analyze how the size of the measured morphological features varies with altitude as well as to understand a genus-specific preference for certain resource types across ant taxa.

The data sets we used can be found here:

‘Ant_traits_in_central_Spain’

‘AntsInGuadarrama’
